\section*{Preamble \& Corrections to Original Formulation}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Preamble \& Corrections}

The original draft was structurally sound but contained one scope error and
several omissions. All issues are flagged with coloured side-bars.
Material not in the original is marked
\textbf{\textcolor{midblue}{ADDED}};
corrected statements are marked
\textbf{\textcolor{accentred}{CORRECTION}}.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Issues found:}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\arabic*.]
  \item \textbf{H2 scope error.} The constraint was attached to
        $\text{tier\_match}(m)=1$, which includes Tier-1 vs Tier-2 matches.
        In the code the prime-time slot requirement fires only when
        \emph{both} teams are individually in~$T_1$.
  \item \textbf{Missing: feasible domain definition.} The set of valid
        (match, slot) pairs was never formally stated.
  \item \textbf{Missing: $\text{load}_w$ definition.} Used in the
        objective without being declared as a variable.
  \item \textbf{Missing: CAF buffer constraint (H12).}
        A hard 4-day exclusion zone around CAF match dates for participating
        teams is a core hard rule.
  \item \textbf{Missing: home/away streak constraint (H13).}
        The maximum-2 consecutive streak rule is enforced by construction in
        the fixture pre-solve.
  \item \textbf{Minor: H8 domain.} The sum in the
        $\text{match\_time}$ definition should be over
        $\text{feasible\_domain}(m)$, not all~$S$.
  \item \textbf{Missing: CAF repair model (Section~8).}
        The post-baseline audit and greedy repair phase had no formulation.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Feasible Domain}

\added{%
  This section was entirely absent from the original. The feasible domain
  determines which (match, slot) pairs are candidates for variable creation.
  Defining it explicitly is essential for a complete formulation.
}

\medskip
Let $\text{feasible\_domain}(m) \subseteq S$ be the set of slots that match
$m$ is \emph{a priori} permitted to use. A slot $s$ belongs to
$\text{feasible\_domain}(m)$ if and only if all of the following hold:

\begin{align}
&(\text{FD-1}) \quad \text{date}(s) \notin \text{FIFA\_DATES} \tag{FD-1}\\
&(\text{FD-2}) \quad \text{date}(s) \text{ has valid date, week, and
  kickoff metadata} \tag{FD-2}\\
&(\text{FD-3}) \quad \forall\, t \in \text{team}(m) \cap C,\;
  \forall\, d \in \text{caf\_dates}(t) : \notag\\
&\phantom{(\text{FD-3}) \quad}
  \bigl|\text{date}(s) - d\bigr| \geq \text{CAF\_BUFFER} \tag{FD-3}
\end{align}

\noindent
If FD-3 would reduce $\text{feasible\_domain}(m)$ to $\varnothing$, the
domain is relaxed to all non-FIFA slots and the CAF audit phase
(Section~\ref{sec:repair}) will detect and repair the violation.

Constraints H3--H11 (rest gaps, venue conflicts, load caps) are enforced
\emph{inside} the CP-SAT model and are \emph{not} applied at
domain-construction time.

\section{Hard Constraints}

\subsection{H1 --- Assignment Uniqueness}
\begin{equation}\tag{H1}
  \sum_{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m)} x_{m,s} = 1,
  \qquad \forall\, m \in M
\end{equation}
Every match is assigned to exactly one slot.

% ── H2 ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{H2 --- Tier-1 Derby Slot Requirement}

\corrected{%
  The original condition was $\text{tier\_match}(m) = 1$. This is too broad:
  $\text{tier\_match}(m) = 1$ also applies to Tier-1 vs Tier-2 matches,
  but the code enforces the prime-time requirement only when
  \emph{both} teams are individually in $T_1$.
  The correct scope is $\text{is\_derby}(m) = 1$.
}

\begin{equation}\tag{H2}
  \sum_{\substack{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m) \\
                  \text{tier\_slot}(s) = 1}}
  x_{m,s} = 1,
  \qquad \forall\, m \in M : \text{is\_derby}(m) = 1
\end{equation}
A match between two Tier-1 teams must be assigned to a Tier-1 (prime-time)
slot.

\subsection{H3 --- Team Date Uniqueness}
\begin{equation}\tag{H3}
  \sum_{\substack{m \,\in\, M \\ t \,\in\, \text{team}(m)}}
  \sum_{\substack{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m) \\
                  \text{date}(s) = d}}
  x_{m,s} \leq 1,
  \qquad \forall\, t \in T,\; \forall\, d \in D
\end{equation}
Each team plays at most one league match per calendar date.

\subsection{H4 --- Venue Slot Uniqueness}
\begin{equation}\tag{H4}
  \sum_{\substack{m \,\in\, M \\ \text{venue}(m) = v}}
  x_{m,s} \leq 1,
  \qquad \forall\, v \in V,\; \forall\, s \in S
\end{equation}
Each venue hosts at most one match per kickoff slot.

\subsection{H5 --- Daily Match Cap}
\begin{equation}\tag{H5}
  \sum_{m \in M}
  \sum_{\substack{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m) \\
                  \text{date}(s) = d}}
  x_{m,s} \leq \text{MAX\_DAY},
  \qquad \forall\, d \in D
\end{equation}
At most $\text{MAX\_DAY} = 3$ league matches may be scheduled on any
one calendar date.

\subsection{H6 --- Slot Concurrency Cap}
\begin{equation}\tag{H6}
  \sum_{m \in M} x_{m,s} \leq \text{MAX\_SLOT},
  \qquad \forall\, s \in S
\end{equation}
At most $\text{MAX\_SLOT} = 2$ matches may share the same exact kickoff slot.

\subsection{H7 --- Team Rest (Sliding 4-Day Window)}
\begin{equation}\tag{H7}
  \sum_{\substack{m \,\in\, M \\ t \,\in\, \text{team}(m)}}
  \sum_{\substack{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m) \\
                  \text{date}(s) \,\in\, [d,\, d + \text{REST\_LOCAL} - 1]}}
  x_{m,s} \leq 1,
  \qquad \forall\, t \in T,\; \forall\, d \in D
\end{equation}
Each team may play at most one league match in any sliding 4-day window
$[d,\, d+3]$, which guarantees a minimum of 3 full rest days between
consecutive matches.

\subsection{H8 --- Match Time Definition}

\corrected{%
  The original sum was written over all $s \in S$. Variables exist only for
  $s \in \text{feasible\_domain}(m)$, so the sum must be restricted
  accordingly.
}

\begin{equation}\tag{H8}
  \text{match\_time}_m
  = \sum_{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m)}
    \text{time\_index}(s)\cdot x_{m,s},
  \qquad \forall\, m \in M
\end{equation}
Because exactly one $x_{m,s}$ equals 1, $\text{match\_time}_m$ equals the
chronological index of the chosen slot.

\subsection{H9 --- Round Min/Max Definition}
\begin{align}\tag{H9a}
  \text{round\_min}_r &= \min_{\substack{m \,\in\, M \\
                             \text{round}(m) = r}} \text{match\_time}_m,
  \qquad \forall\, r \in R \\[4pt]\tag{H9b}
  \text{round\_max}_r &= \max_{\substack{m \,\in\, M \\
                             \text{round}(m) = r}} \text{match\_time}_m,
  \qquad \forall\, r \in R
\end{align}
These are encoded in CP-SAT via \texttt{AddMinEquality} and
\texttt{AddMaxEquality}.

\subsection{H10 --- Strict Round Ordering}
\begin{equation}\tag{H10}
  \text{round\_max}_r + 1 \leq \text{round\_min}_{r+1},
  \qquad \forall\, r \in \{1, 2, \dots, 33\}
\end{equation}
All matches of round $r$ must finish strictly before the first match of
round $r+1$. Ordering is on $\text{time\_index}$ integers, not raw dates.

\subsection{H11 --- Weekly Hard Cap}
\begin{equation}\tag{H11}
  \sum_{m \in M}
  \sum_{\substack{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m) \\
                  \text{week}(s) = w}}
  x_{m,s} \leq \text{HARD\_MAX\_WEEK},
  \qquad \forall\, w \in W
\end{equation}
No more than $\text{HARD\_MAX\_WEEK} = 18$ league matches may be
scheduled in any calendar week.

% ── H12 (ADDED) ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{H12 --- CAF Buffer (Bidirectional)}

\added{%
  This constraint was entirely absent from the original. It is a hard rule
  enforced first as a domain pre-filter (FD-3) and verified definitively
  in the post-baseline audit.
}

\begin{equation}\tag{H12}
  \sum_{\substack{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m) \\
                  |\,\text{date}(s) - d\,| < \text{CAF\_BUFFER}}}
  x_{m,s} = 0,
  \qquad
  \forall\, m \in M,\;
  t \in \text{team}(m) \cap C,\;
  d \in \text{caf\_dates}(t)
\end{equation}
A match involving a CAF-participating team may not be scheduled within
$\text{CAF\_BUFFER} = 4$ calendar days of that team's CAF match date.
The buffer is \emph{bidirectional}: it applies both before and after the
CAF date.

% ── H13 (ADDED) ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{H13 --- Home/Away Streak Limit}

\added{%
  This constraint was absent from the original. It is enforced by construction
  in a dedicated fixture-orientation CP-SAT pre-solve (not as a variable
  constraint in the main model). It is stated here for completeness.
}

No team may have more than $\text{MAX\_STREAK} = 2$ consecutive home matches
or 2 consecutive away matches across the 34-round sequence:
\begin{equation}\tag{H13}
  \forall\, t \in T,\; \forall\, r \in \{1,\dots,32\}: \quad
  \sum_{k=0}^{2} \mathbf{1}\!\bigl[\text{side}(t, r+k) = \sigma\bigr] \leq 2,
  \qquad \sigma \in \{\text{H}, \text{A}\}
\end{equation}
where $\text{side}(t, r)$ denotes whether team $t$ is home (H) or away (A) in
round $r$. This is satisfied by the fixture pre-solve before the main model
runs, so the main model receives a fixed, streak-compliant fixture list.

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Objective Function}

Minimize the weighted penalty:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{Z} = \mathcal{Z}_1 + \mathcal{Z}_2 + \mathcal{Z}_3
              + \mathcal{Z}_4 + \mathcal{Z}_5
\end{equation}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Term 1 --- Round placement deviation:}
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{Z}_1 = W_{\text{ROUND\_ORDER}}
    \sum_{m \in M}
    \sum_{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m)}
    \bigl|\text{week}(s) - \text{nominal\_week}(\text{round}(m))\bigr|
    \cdot x_{m,s}
\end{equation}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Term 2 --- Weekly underload penalty:}
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{Z}_2 = W_{\text{WEEK\_UNDERLOAD}}
    \sum_{w \in W} \text{under\_load}_w
\end{equation}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Term 3 --- Weekly overload penalty:}
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{Z}_3 = W_{\text{WEEK\_OVERLOAD}}
    \sum_{w \in W} \text{over\_load}_w
\end{equation}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Term 4 --- Travel distance:}
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{Z}_4 = W_{\text{TRAVEL}}
    \sum_{m \in M}
    \sum_{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m)}
    \text{travel\_km}(m) \cdot x_{m,s}
\end{equation}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Term 5 --- Tier mismatch:}
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{Z}_5 = W_{\text{TIER\_MISMATCH}}
    \sum_{m \in M}
    \sum_{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m)}
    \bigl|\text{tier\_match}(m) - \text{tier\_slot}(s)\bigr|
    \cdot x_{m,s}
\end{equation}

% ── Load deviation definitions ───────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Weekly Load Auxiliary Definitions}

\added{%
  $\text{load}_w$ was used in the original without formal definition.
}

\begin{equation}
  \text{load}_w
  = \sum_{m \in M}
    \sum_{\substack{s \,\in\, \text{feasible\_domain}(m) \\
                    \text{week}(s) = w}}
    x_{m,s},
  \qquad \forall\, w \in W
\end{equation}

The deviation variables are linearisations of the $\max(0,\cdot)$ operator:
\begin{alignat}{3}
  \text{under\_load}_w &\geq \text{SOFT\_MIN\_WEEK} - \text{load}_w, &\quad&
  \text{under\_load}_w \geq 0, &\quad& \forall\, w \in W \\[4pt]
  \text{over\_load}_w  &\geq \text{load}_w - \text{SOFT\_MAX\_WEEK}, &\quad&
  \text{over\_load}_w  \geq 0, &\quad& \forall\, w \in W
\end{alignat}
Since the objective minimises $\text{under\_load}_w$ and $\text{over\_load}_w$,
the solver sets:
\[
  \text{under\_load}_w = \max\bigl(0,\; \text{SOFT\_MIN\_WEEK} - \text{load}_w\bigr),
  \qquad
  \text{over\_load}_w  = \max\bigl(0,\; \text{load}_w - \text{SOFT\_MAX\_WEEK}\bigr).
\]

\section{Post-Baseline CAF Audit \& Repair Model}
\label{sec:repair}

\added{%
  This section was entirely absent from the original. The system uses a
  two-phase approach: the baseline model above deliberately ignores CAF
  constraints to maximise schedule quality; a separate audit and greedy
  repair pass then resolves any violations.
}

\subsection{Audit Criterion}

After the baseline is solved, a scheduled match $m$ assigned to slot $s$ is
flagged as a \emph{CAF violation} if any team $t \in \text{team}(m) \cap C$
satisfies any of:

\begin{align*}
  &\text{(SAME\_DAY)}\quad \text{date}(s) \in \text{caf\_dates}(t) \\[4pt]
  &\text{(PRE)}\quad
    \exists\, d \in \text{caf\_dates}(t) :
    0 < d - \text{date}(s) < \text{CAF\_BUFFER} \\[4pt]
  &\text{(POST)}\quad
    \exists\, d \in \text{caf\_dates}(t) :
    0 < \text{date}(s) - d < \text{CAF\_BUFFER}
\end{align*}

Violating matches are removed from the accepted baseline and queued for repair.
Accepted matches become \textbf{fixed} in the repair phase.

\subsection{Repair Feasibility Conditions}

A slot $s$ is a valid repair slot for queued match $q$ if and only if all of
the following hold:

\begin{align*}
  &(\text{R1})\quad \text{date}(s) \geq \text{date}(s_{\text{original}})
    \tag{no backward reschedule} \\[2pt]
  &(\text{R2})\quad \text{date\_load}[\text{date}(s)] < \text{MAX\_DAY} \\[2pt]
  &(\text{R3})\quad \text{date}(s) \notin \text{existing dates of }
    \text{home}(q) \cup \text{existing dates of } \text{away}(q) \\[2pt]
  &(\text{R4})\quad |\text{date}(s) - d'| \geq \text{REST\_LOCAL}
    \quad \forall\, d' \in \text{existing match dates of each team in team}(q) \\[2pt]
  &(\text{R5})\quad \text{slot\_usage}[s] = 0 \\[2pt]
  &(\text{R6})\quad \text{inserting } s \text{ does not create a streak}
    > \text{MAX\_STREAK}
    \text{ for either team} \\[2pt]
  &(\text{R7})\quad |\text{date}(s) - d| \geq \text{CAF\_BUFFER}
    \quad \forall\, t \in \text{team}(q) \cap C,\;
    d \in \text{caf\_dates}(t) \\[2pt]
  &(\text{R8})\quad \text{week\_load}[\text{week}(s)] < \text{HARD\_MAX\_WEEK}
\end{align*}

\subsection{Repair Objective}

Repair is solved greedily (most-constrained match first, nearest-date
preference). Among all slots satisfying R1--R8, the chosen slot is:
\[
  s^* = \arg\min_{s \;\text{satisfying R1--R8}}
        \bigl|\text{date}(s) - \text{date}(s_{\text{original}})\bigr|
\]
Up to three greedy passes are executed. Matches that cannot be placed in any
